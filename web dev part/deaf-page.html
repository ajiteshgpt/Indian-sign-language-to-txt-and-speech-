Empowering the Deaf Community through awareness and education.

About the Deaf Community

The deaf community is a group of individuals who use sign language as their primary means of communication. They share a rich culture and heritage, with a unique set of values, norms, and social structures. Deaf culture emphasizes visual communication and the importance of deaf identity and pride.

A Brief History

Deaf history traces back to the early days of civilization, with the first recorded instances of sign language emerging in ancient times. Throughout the centuries, the deaf community has faced challenges, from discrimination to exclusion. However, with the advent of formal sign languages, educational institutions for the deaf, and activism, the community has made great strides in asserting their rights and establishing a strong cultural identity.
